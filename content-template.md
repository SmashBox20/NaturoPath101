## Content Standards

### Writing Guidelines

1. **Tone**: Professional, compassionate, accessible
2. **Reading Level**: 8th grade (use Flesch-Kincaid test)
3. **Person**: Second person ("you") for engaging readers
4. **Tense**: Present tense for current information
5. **Voice**: Active voice preferred over passive

### Evidence Standards

1. **Prioritize**: Peer-reviewed research, systematic reviews, meta-analyses
2. **Accept**: Clinical trials, observational studies, traditional use documentation
3. **Note limitations**: When evidence is limited or contradictory
4. **Date**: Prefer recent studies (<10 years) when available
5. **Quality**: Assess study design, sample size, conflicts of interest

### Safety Standards

1. **Always include**: Contraindications, interactions, warnings
2. **Be specific**: About populations at risk (pregnancy, children, etc.)
3. **Emphasize**: Importance of medical supervision for serious conditions
4. **Highlight**: Emergency warning signs prominently
5. **Update**: Safety information as new data emerges

### Image Standards

1. **Quality**: High resolution (min 1200px width)
2. **Rights**: Only use properly licensed images
3. **Attribution**: Credit photographers/sources
4. **Alt text**: Descriptive for accessibility
5. **Relevance**: Must accurately represent the plant/condition

### Review Process

1. **Peer Review**: All content reviewed by at least one other team member
2. **Medical Review**: Healthcare professional must approve
3. **Legal Review**: For sensitive content or liability concerns
4. **Editorial Review**: For consistency and quality
5. **Annual Review**: All content reviewed annually for updates
